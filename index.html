What this is

A coding aid for contrived free-operant duration recording — a standard ABA measurement procedure where a child is free to engage with a chosen set of arranged activities, and the observer captures how long each engagement lasts.

Designed for the post-hoc step: you have the session on video, and you need to play it back and quantify engagement.

What it measures

  • Total duration per activity
  • Frequency — number of engagement bouts per activity
  • Per-bout duration with start/end timestamps in video time
  • Speed-adjusted playback — watch at 1.25× / 1.5× / 2×; totals reflect real engagement duration

How it works

Each activity is a large tile, split into two tap zones — designed for blind operation while your eyes stay on the video:

+ Left half — toggle
Start or stop this activity. Other running activities continue. Use when behaviors overlap.
▶ Right half — solo
Stop everything else and run only this one. Use when the child cleanly switches.
■ Bottom bar — idle
Stop all activities. Child is doing nothing of interest.
1× Top pill — speed
Cycle 1× → 1.25× → 1.5× → 1.75× → 2×. Splits ongoing bouts so totals stay accurate.

Distinct haptic patterns confirm every action so you don't have to look at the phone.

Why it helps

  • Multiple simultaneous timers — most stopwatches handle one channel at a time
  • Automatic speed scaling — no spreadsheet arithmetic at the end
  • Multi-session history — every video gets its own record, saved locally
  • CSV export — long-format rows ready for Excel / Sheets / SPSS
